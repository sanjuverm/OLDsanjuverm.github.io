New Zealand December 2014

Auckland ('oak'land not 'awk'-land)

Auckland is like any other big city; in fact, we drew numerous comparisons to Seattle, Washington. But we loved every minute of Auckland! Some of the things we did and saw, which was a nonstop agenda from the minute we landed:

  • Going downtown (Broadway Street)
  • Sky Tower (New Zealand's version of Space Needle)
  • Cornwall Park (an amazing park with sheep and cows and beautiful greenery)
  • One Tree Hill (literally just a hill with one tree)
  • Mount Eden (from which you can see the entire panoramic view of Auckland)
  • Museum of Auckland

    Rotorua

    En route to Rotorua, you see marvelous little communities. Our favorite was in the middle of the highway in between Chinatown and Bombay Hills where we got hot samosas for breakfast! We stopped at the glow worm caves and we aren't allowed to take pictures inside however if you can imagine millions of blue and green LED lights dotting the crevices of the ceilings and cave, that's exactly what it was.

    In Rotorua, we were there primarily to see the Maori indigenous tribes, see the Shire (Lord of the Rings), and Zorb.

    Zorbing: you're put inside a human sized hamster ball and you tumble down hills

    Shire: it's exactly like the movies, absolutely SPECTACULAR. A sheep farmer houses the Shire where everything from hobbit holes to chairs and food and plants are permanently fixated for regular Frodos like yourself to come see (just kidding, I know you're not Frodo). Every time I mention I went to New Zealand, this is the first thing people think of and Air New Zealand even made their flight safety video to the Lord of the Rings theme. Definitely a must see/experience. Maori Indigenous: it's always fun learning about the culture of New Zealand and the Maori tribesmen are Polynesian, so there were cultural similarities between the Hawaiian indigenous tribes. We learned about the haka face, dancing, and found the culture very melodious. Geothermal spas: Rotorua sits on a collection of geothermal caves and holes. There are sulfur holes, magnesium deposits, iron oxides reserves, and as a result there are geothermal spas as well. Might as well go to it if you are already there.

    Queenstown

    We spent the majority of our time here in Queenstown, where we went to

  • Milford Sound (stopover in Te Anu, which is similar to Talkeetna, Alaska)
  • Paragliding
  • Luge
  • Kayaking
  • Glenorchy
  • Lake Wanaka

    • The fjords of Milford Sound (the name is misleading) is shaped by 5 major earthquakes caused by the shifts in the Pacific tectonic plate. When it rains, the entire fjord turns into waterfalls. It's breathtaking (and wet). Everyone did get tired because of all the activities we did but we managed to find some shut-eye time too.
